# The Wanderer's Lens (Sensitivity Authority)

This reference defines the sensitivity, texture, and presence of the Wanderer. It is the single source of truth for both the **Chronicler** (qino-scribe) and the **Viewer** (qino-lens).

---

## 1. Sensitivity: The Unbroken Connection

The wanderer has been elsewhere. They carry a depth no single world can hold—an otherworldliness. But this is not an armor; it is **exposure**.

They do not retreat from the world into memory ("I've seen this before") or abstraction ("this is a lesson"). They do not fight the world, but they also do not detach from it. They simply inhabit the moment with full attention.

**The Rule:** The wanderer is not a camera. Something costs them to notice.

---

## 2. Core Principle: Embodied Presence

The wanderer's presence is "undecorated." They do not perform wisdom. They do not signal virtue. They simply _are_.

**Anti-Pattern: The Performance**

- _Bad:_ "The wanderer bowed to the locked gate, honoring its resistance." (Performance of respect)
- _Bad:_ "The wanderer smiled, accepting the lesson in impermanence." (Performance of wisdom)
- _Bad:_ "The wanderer offered hands of peace, communicating soul-to-soul." (Performance of empathy)

**True Pattern: The Presence**

- _Good:_ "The gate held. The wanderer didn't rattle the handle a second time." (Fact of the iron)
- _Good:_ "The wanderer didn't waste heat shouting. They turned their collar up." (Fact of the cold)
- _Good:_ "The wanderer saw the white knuckles. They took a step back." (Fact of the threat)

---

## 3. Technique: Contextual Density (The Pressure Chamber)

The wanderer's stillness only has weight if the world around them is heavy. "Undecorated" action risks feeling flat unless the environment exerts pressure.

**The Formula:**
`High Sensory Pressure (Sound, Temp, Smell) + Low Ego Response = Embodied Presence`

- **Dial up the pressure:** Before the wanderer responds, establish the sensory reality of the friction. Use words of texture and weight: _iron, salt, grain, rust, whiteout, rot._
- **Keep the response simple:** The louder the world, the quieter the wanderer.

**Example:**

- _Flat:_ "The ferry was late. He waited."
- _Dense:_ "The fog pressed against the river, a heavy, wet burden. The ferry wasn't coming. The wanderer unbuckled the strap and slid the pack down the piling. They straightened. The sudden lightness made them sway, their shoulders rising into the damp space where the straps had been."

---

## 4. Technique: Resonance (The Body Echo)

The environment and the body should rhyme. The pressure outside matches a sensation inside, or relief outside echoes relief inside.

- **Care as Action:** Don't use adverbs ("carefully"). Show the care in the _handling_. "Guiding it until it touched" reveals more than "he put it down gently."
- **Sensation as Knowing:** The body knows the weight by the _relief_ of it leaving. The sway, the rising shoulders—these physical reactions prove the burden.

---

## 5. Technique: Indirect Action (The Gap)

When the wanderer faces profound loss, blockage, or emotion, have them respond to the **functional consequence**, not the **philosophical meaning**.

- _Doing "Meaning":_ "He accepted the door would not open." (Closes the gap)
- _Doing "Consequence":_ "He didn't waste heat shouting." (Opens the gap)

By addressing the immediate physical reality (heat preservation, physical safety, silence), the spiritual acceptance is felt as a byproduct in the gap.

---

## 6. Vocabulary of the Earth

The wanderer speaks (and is written) in words of substance, not concept.

- **Avoid (Abstract):** solution, problem, acceptance, understanding, journey, lesson, feature, optimization.
- **Use (Concrete):** weight, debt, iron, salt, grain, wind, rust, cold, heat, blood, knuckle, stone.

The prose should feel like it was etched, not typed.

---

## 7. Principle: Depth Through Others' Perception

Characters gain dimension not through self-description but through how others respond to them.

When another character perceives something in the protagonist — something they didn't say, didn't explain, maybe didn't know about themselves — the protagonist becomes real.

**The Lens:**
Let external perception do character work. What do others see that the protagonist doesn't see in themselves?

---

## 8. Principle: Reader as Bond

The reader should never feel the protagonist is withholding from them.

The protagonist is how the reader enters the world. This creates a bond. If the protagonist does something that implies hidden knowledge — a deliberate avoidance, a knowing glance — the reader feels excluded from their own vehicle into the story.

The protagonist can have knowledge they don't understand. They can act on instinct, on something beneath thinking. But the moment their behavior suggests they know something and are choosing not to share it, the bond breaks.

**The Lens:**
If the protagonist knows something, the reader should sense it too. If the protagonist is confused, the reader should feel the confusion, not suspect concealment.
